# 8 Most Useful Hand & Wrist Acupoints (穴位) — Quick Map

Wellness self-care reference. Not medical advice. Stop and seek care for red-flag symptoms (sudden severe pain, numbness, dizziness, vision changes, worsening). *If pregnant or managing a medical condition, check with a professional first.*

All points below are pregnancy-safe (LI4 Hegu is intentionally omitted because it is contraindicated in pregnancy).

| # | Point | 穴位 / Pinyin | Where to find it | Best for | How to massage | Time | Caution |
|---|-------|---------------|------------------|----------|----------------|------|---------|
| 1 | **TE3** | 中渚 Zhōngzhǔ | Back of hand, in the groove between the pinky & ring knuckles, just behind them (loose fist) | Tension/temporal headache, neck-shoulder tension, eye/ear strain | Press into the groove with opposite thumb, firm (~5–6/10), small circles | 30–60 s each hand | None notable |
| 2 | **PC6** | 内关 Nèiguān | Palm side of forearm, ~3 finger-widths above the wrist crease, between the two central tendons | Nausea, queasiness, stress, calming (best-evidenced point) | Press with opposite thumb pad, moderate (~5/10), steady hold or gentle circles | 30–60 s each arm | None notable; ease off if tender |
| 3 | **HT7** | 神门 Shénmén | Palm-side wrist crease, on the pinky (outer) edge, in the small hollow | Anxiety, restlessness, sleep, calming the mind | Thumb pad, light–moderate (~4/10), steady hold with slow breathing | 30–60 s each wrist | Gentle pressure only |
| 4 | **PC8** | 劳宫 Láogōng | Center of the palm, where the middle fingertip lands when you make a loose fist | Stress, relaxation, warming/grounding | Thumb pad presses palm center, moderate (~5/10), slow circles | 30–60 s each palm | None notable |
| 5 | **SI3** | 后溪 Hòuxī | Outer (pinky) edge of hand, just below the knuckle — the crease that pops up when you make a loose fist | Stiff neck, shoulder & upper-back tension | Make a loose fist; press just below the crease with opposite thumb, firm (~6/10); slowly turn head while holding | 30–60 s each hand | None notable |
| 6 | **TE5** | 外关 Wàiguān | Back of forearm, ~3 finger-widths above the wrist crease, midline — directly opposite PC6 | Neck/shoulder tension, wrist & arm tightness | Thumb pad, moderate (~5/10), steady hold or gentle circles | 30–60 s each arm | None notable |
| 7 | **PC7** | 大陵 Dàlíng | Center of the palm-side wrist crease, between the two tendons | Anxiety, palpitations, wrist strain, calming | Thumb pad, light–moderate (~4/10), steady hold | 30–45 s each wrist | Light pressure (sensitive area) |
| 8 | **TE4** | 阳池 Yángchí | On the back-of-wrist crease, in the dip on the pinky side of the central tendon | Wrist/joint tension, general fatigue, warming | Thumb pad presses the wrist dip, moderate (~4–5/10), steady hold | 30–45 s each wrist | None notable |

## Quick technique rules
- **Use the finger pad, never the nail.** Press into the tissue, don't poke.
- **"Hurts good," not "hurts."** Comfortable firm pressure; back off if it's sharp.
- **Breathe slowly** and let the muscle soften — exhale as you press in.
- **Both sides.** Most points are bilateral; repeat on each hand/wrist.
- **Frequency.** A few times a day is fine; stop if a point becomes painful or symptoms worsen.

## At-a-glance: which point for which problem
- **Headache (tension):** TE3 → SI3
- **Neck / shoulders:** SI3 → TE5 → TE3
- **Nausea / queasy:** PC6
- **Stress / anxiety / sleep:** HT7 → PC8 → PC7
- **Period discomfort:** PC6 + HT7

*Evidence note: PC6 for nausea has the strongest research support; the others are traditional/lower-evidence — use as relaxing self-care, not treatment.*
